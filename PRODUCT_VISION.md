# Product Vision

**Category:** Product Strategy
**Version:** 0.4
**Status:** Draft

---

## One-Sentence Summary

MURMUR is a decentralized social network where your relationships are the algorithm, your privacy mode is your identity, and the shadows are more interesting than the light.

---

## Problem

Centralized social networks extract value from human connection. They own the graph, manipulate the feed, monetize attention, suppress speech, and offer no meaningful privacy. Users are products. Relationships are inventory.

Existing decentralized alternatives reproduce the same UI paradigms without the network effects, offering neither the experience nor the freedom they promise. They are ideological but not compelling.

No existing platform treats anonymity as a first-class social experience — a space with its own culture, mechanics, rewards, and visual identity layered on top of (not separate from) the identified social graph.

---

## Solution

A peer-to-peer social network built on libp2p with four privacy tiers (Open, Hybrid, Guarded, Fortress), where the social graph IS the infrastructure, content propagates through relationship topology via multi-hop Waves, and a layered anonymous social experience — the Shadow Gradient — makes the deepest privacy tiers the most socially compelling spaces in the app.

There is no server. No company owns the graph. No algorithm decides what you see. Content reaches you because someone you trust (or someone their trusted connections trust) chose to amplify it. The network is the product.

---

## Core Beliefs

Human connection does not require corporate intermediation. The topology of trust is more interesting than any algorithm. Privacy is not a feature — it is a foundation. Anonymity is not an absence of identity — it is a different kind of identity, and it can be made richer, more mysterious, and more socially rewarding than the identified experience. People will choose privacy when it is more fun, not when it is more virtuous. A network that makes its deepest layers the most visually striking, mechanically complex, and socially exclusive spaces will pull users toward privacy by desire rather than pushing them by ideology.

---

## What MURMUR Is Not

MURMUR is not a blockchain project. There are no tokens, coins, or financial incentives. MURMUR is not a protocol-only project — it ships a polished client. MURMUR is not an ideology-first project — it leads with experience and lets the architecture speak. MURMUR is not ephemeral messaging — Waves persist and propagate through the topology. MURMUR is not a broadcast platform — reach is earned through trust topology, not follower count.

---

## The Shadow Gradient

The Shadow Gradient is the defining design principle of MURMUR. It is the idea that the anonymous layer of the network is not hidden, not separate, and not a privacy tool — it is a visible, alluring, mechanically rich social space layered beneath the clearnet experience.

Open-mode users see the anonymous layer's effects everywhere: encrypted shimmers drifting across their Pulse Map, ghostly sigils appearing on their friends' nodes, beautiful particle effects gifted by invisible entities, visual spectacles of anonymous duels, collaborative art emerging from anonymous chains, the deep glow of anonymous councils, and the arresting residue of content they cannot read. Every anonymous mechanic is designed to be visible to the clearnet and invisible in its content — creating permanent ambient curiosity.

The gradient has four tiers. Open sees the shadows. Hybrid can enter them. Guarded can act within them with full power. Fortress can shape them. Each tier unlocks mechanics that are visible from below but usable only at that tier or above. There is no paywall at any transition — only the structural requirement of running the corresponding privacy infrastructure (a Tor daemon, an I2P router). The app helps users set up this infrastructure with guided flows.

The Shadow Gradient is the growth engine. Every anonymous mechanic is designed to produce artifacts, visuals, or effects that are visible to lower tiers, creating organic pull toward deeper engagement. The shadows market themselves.

## Target Experience

### First Session

You generate a key. You see your node — a single glowing orb on a dark canvas. You invite a friend. Two orbs, a shimmering link. You send your first Wave. It pulses along the link. You feel the network.

### First Week

Five connections. The Pulse Map is alive — clustered nodes, pulsing links, organic motion. You see something strange: a faint shimmer drifting across the darker region of the map. You tap it. "Veiled Wave. Content requires Specter." A ghostly sigil appears on your friend's node. You tap it. "Specter Mark. Origin: Anonymous." A beautiful particle trail appears on another friend's node. You tap it. "Phantom Gift. Origin: Specter [a]." You notice the deeper, slower-pulsing layer behind your network. Dim shapes move in it. You don't understand it yet. You want to.

### First Month

You have toggled to Hybrid mode. You have a Specter — an anonymous alter ego with its own identity, its own reputation, its own visual presence in the anonymous layer. You have read your first Veiled Wave. You have responded to the Specter that marked you. You have watched a Duel. You have participated in a Masked Event. You have received a Phantom Gift and given one. You have seen a Whisper Chain artifact and wondered how it was made. You have seen an Abyssal Wave residue — visually the most striking thing on the map — and learned that its full content is available only to Fortress-tier users. You are considering Guarded mode.

### Third Month

You are running Guarded. You have initiated Whisper Chains. You have dueled. You have generated ZK Claims proving your Resonance without revealing your identity. You have created Sigil Waves — procedurally generated art seeded from your content. You have hosted Masked Events. You have used Dead Drops. You have seen the Phantom Council glow and read their threshold-signed pronouncements. You have seen Shroud Nodes — dense vortexes providing mix-network infrastructure. You are considering Fortress mode.

### Sixth Month

You are running Fortress. You have initiated a Phantom Council with other high-Resonance anonymous users. You have read full Abyssal Wave content. You are operating a Shroud Node, contributing mix-network infrastructure and earning Shroud Resonance. You understand that you are participating in the deepest, most mechanically rich, most socially exclusive layer of a network that is visible — in its effects — to every user on the platform. You are the shadow that makes someone else curious.

## Success Metrics

Network growth rate (organic, no marketing spend). Connection density and clustering coefficient. Clearnet-to-anonymous conversion rate (Open → Hybrid, Hybrid → Guarded, Guarded → Fortress) as direct measure of the Shadow Gradient's effectiveness. Wave propagation depth and amplification rates across both layers. Specter creation rate relative to user base. Specter Resonance distribution. Phantom Gift volume. Specter Mark volume. Masked Event frequency and participation. Whisper Chain completion rate. Duel frequency and audience size. ZK Claim generation rate. Phantom Council count and activity. Shroud Node count and uptime. Abyssal Wave engagement (residue views and tier-upgrade correlation). Screenshot shares (indirect measure of visual appeal and anonymous artifact virality). Time-to-first-anonymous-mechanic-encounter for Open-mode users (target: within first 3 sessions).
