```md
MURMUR GAME LIBRARY — CONSOLIDATED CATALOG v4.0
================================================

50 titles, organized into 8 discovery shelves.
AR-AUGMENTED entries marked with ▲ and include an AR LAYER note
describing the optional augmented-reality mode. AR is always
OPT-IN and every AR-enabled title remains fully playable without
a headset or phone-AR pass-through.

AR design principles applied:
  - AR layer must not require revealing physical location to peers
  - Anchors are relative (to a room, a surface, a body) not
    geographic (no GPS broadcast)
  - AR never becomes the only input modality — text/tap fallback
    always works
  - No always-on camera streams to peers; pose data only
  - Titles selected for AR treatment are ones where spatial
    presence genuinely changes the experience, not novelty paint

Shelf legend:
  [A] Ambient & Social        [S] Social Deduction & Party
  [N] Narrative & RPG         [P] Puzzle & Solo
  [X] Abstract Strategy       [E] Economy & 4X
  [C] Cards & Tricks          [W] Worlds & Persistent
```

```md
SHELF [A] — AMBIENT & SOCIAL
=============================

1. DEAD DROP
   Async · 1:1 or group · social
   Leave a short message tagged by mood/color/time; a stranger
   stumbles on it later via matching tags. Messages-in-bottles
   for an anonymous network.

12. DREAM JOURNAL
    Async · group (open) · social
    Submit a dream daily; system pairs yours with a stranger's
    and asks you both to name the shared symbol. Agreement
    earns Resonance.

▲ 13. CONSPIRACY BOARD
   Async · group (3–8) · social
   Shared canvas of clippings, stock photos, overheard quotes.
   Players draw red strings between items, add margin notes. At
   session end, the group reads the conspiracy aloud.
   AR LAYER: Pin the conspiracy board to a physical wall in your
   own space. Walk up to clippings to read detail; the red
   strings render as volumetric yarn between anchor points. Your
   annotations appear to other players only on the virtual
   board, not in your room — no spatial audio, no video of your
   space. Flagship AR use case.

15. LAST WORDS
   Async · 1:1 · social
   Exactly 7 messages each, max 140 chars, over 7 days. After
   the 14th, the thread is sealed forever. Structured intimacy
   for strangers.

16. BUREAU OF LOST THINGS
   Async · group (open) · social · FLAGSHIP
   Submit descriptions of things you've lost — objects, moments,
   people, real or fictional. Others "return" items via short
   finding-stories you can accept or decline. Emotional
   archaeology.

17. BROADCAST
   Sync · group (any) · skill+social
   One DJ queues short text "tracks" for 30 min; listeners
   react with a fixed emoji palette; listeners whose reactions
   best match the DJ's hidden favorites win.

▲ 19. QUIET ROOM
   Sync · group (4–12) · social
   20 minutes, no text, only a shared canvas where each player
   controls one small shape. Movements, gatherings, partings.
   One sentence each at the end, revealed simultaneously.
   AR LAYER: Shapes become softly-glowing motes floating in your
   physical room. You move your mote by tilting your head or
   moving a finger. Others' motes appear relative to a shared
   virtual center, not your actual space. Ambient, meditative.

▲ 41. ECHOES
   Async · solo with ambient presence · social · FLAGSHIP
   Walk through a procedurally-painted landscape. Brief non-
   verbal encounters with other travelers. Leave small artifacts
   — a stone, a song-fragment — for future passersby.
   AR LAYER: The landscape overlays onto a walk in your own
   physical environment. Artifacts anchor to surfaces you
   choose; only the abstract surface type ("flat ground,"
   "vertical edge") is shared with peers, never geometry or
   image data. The walk can be paced or stationary. This is the
   AR flagship: a game whose entire point — transient shared
   presence — becomes profound with embodied space.
```

```md
SHELF [S] — SOCIAL DEDUCTION & PARTY
=====================================

2. TELEPHONE MUSEUM
   Async · group (4–12) · social · FLAGSHIP
   Caption → drawing → caption chain. Full distortion revealed
   at the end.

4. RUMOR MILL
   Async · group (6–20) · social
   Two truths and a lie, but each round a randomly selected
   "gossip" can plant a fake fourth option. Scoring rewards
   deception AND detection.

▲ 9. MASK PARTY
   Sync · group (8–16) · social · FLAGSHIP
   Each player assigned a random persona; 15 minutes of free
   conversation; guess each other's personas at the end.
   AR LAYER: Your chosen persona renders as a symbolic mask
   floating in the conversational space. Players reach toward
   a mask to "address" that persona. Voice (if enabled) is
   always pitch-shifted; no faces, no rooms — pure masked
   presence. Anonymity is preserved and enhanced.

24. HIDDEN AGENDA
   Async or sync · group (6–14) · social · FLAGSHIP
   Hidden-role social deduction. Async 18h day phases or live
   45-min sessions. Werewolf/Resistance lineage — and the
   anonymous platform is its ideal home.

32. CODED CLUES
   Sync (20m) or async (24h rounds) · group (4–8) · social
   One-word-clue word association. Spymaster gives a single
   clue to steer teammates to the right words. Codenames lineage.

▲ 46. PIXEL DUEL
   Sync · group (4–10) · social
   Draw the prompt; others guess. Rapid rounds, vote on favorite.
   AR LAYER: Draw in 3D space in front of you with finger or
   stylus. Your sculpture rotates for viewers. Fallback to 2D
   canvas is always one tap away.

47. STORYBOARD
   Async · group (4–7) · social
   Choose an evocative image, write a short clue. Others pick
   images matching your clue. Dixit scoring — be guessed by
   some but not all.
```

```md
SHELF [N] — NARRATIVE & RPG
============================

3. CIPHER DATE
   Sync · 1:1 · social
   Ten-minute conversation where every Nth word encodes a hidden
   sentence via a shared one-time pad. The "date" is the decode.

5. GHOST WRITER
   Async · 1:1 · skill+social
   Collaborative fiction, one sentence each, no edits. After 20
   sentences the story is bound.

10. COLD CASE
    Async · group (4–6) · skill
    One player writes a fictional mystery with 5 clues;
    detectives each ask 3 questions, submit theories at 48h.

18. FORKED PATHS
    Async · group (3–10) · social
    Choose-your-own-adventure. Each player writes one branch
    point; readers vote canon. Non-canon branches persist as
    alternate universes.

▲ 25. DUNGEON CRAWL
    Async · group (3–5 + GM) · skill+social · FLAGSHIP
    Play-by-post tabletop RPG with platform-provided dice,
    sheets, fog-of-war maps. Campaigns span weeks or months.
    AR LAYER: Pull the fog-of-war map onto your coffee table.
    Tokens stand on the surface at scale; rotate the map with a
    gesture. GM can reveal rooms by "lifting" fog panels.
    Crucially, the AR layer is purely personal — other players
    don't see your table, only your moves on the virtual map.

33. WITNESS TABLE
    Async · group (3–7) · skill+social
    Procedurally-generated mystery. Investigate locations,
    share a shared notebook, race to solve. One "Ghost" holds
    the solution per strict release rules.

34. THE HEIST
    Async · group (3–5) · social+skill
    Light caper RPG. Plan via shared doc, execute with
    flashback mechanics — declare prep retroactively, spend a
    resource, roll. One heist per week.
```

```md
SHELF [P] — PUZZLE & SOLO
==========================

36. ROGUE COURIER
    Async solo + leaderboard · skill
    Turn-based solo dungeon runs. Daily/weekly shared seeds;
    ghost replays of top runs viewable. No turn timer.

39. MASTERMIND'S ROOM
    Async · 1:1 or daily solo · skill
    Daily code-breaking puzzle shared globally; head-to-head
    races on a shared solution; craftable puzzles for Whisper
    contacts.

40. CROSSWORD COOPERATIVE
    Async · group (open) or solo · skill
    Daily crossword, plus "relay" mode where three anonymous
    strangers each solve a third; puzzle completes only if all
    three finish by midnight.

▲ 44. THE OBSERVATORY
    Async solo + asymmetric pairs · 1:1 or solo · skill
    Static puzzle-box world, rotated monthly. Solve alone, or
    pair with a player holding different clues. Hints are
    shareable; full answers are not.
    AR LAYER: The puzzle box materializes as a rotatable object
    on a surface of your choosing. Inspect from every angle,
    open compartments by reaching into them. Paired partner
    sees the same box in their own space and can gesture at
    parts you both see. Myst-lineage spatial puzzles shine here.

49. FIELD MANUAL
    Async · group (3–6) · skill
    One player invents a secret rule; others propose examples;
    rule-holder marks them following or breaking. Induce the
    rule. Zendo lineage.
```

```md
SHELF [W] — WORLDS & PERSISTENT
================================

▲ 6. FREQUENCY
   Sync · group (4–8) · social
   A concept sits on a 1–10 dial visible only to the tuner.
   Teams guess the dial position from a one-word clue.
   AR LAYER: The dial manifests as a physical-feeling hand-crank
   gauge. Clue-giver turns it with a gesture; only they can see
   the needle. Lightweight shared object, no spatial data shared.

8. SIGNAL / NOISE
   Sync · group (6–10) · skill
   Coordinate an ordering of 5 items using only preset emoji
   reactions — no text. Anonymous cooperation puzzle.

▲ 14. THE BOTANIST
   Async solo-with-social · skill
   Tend an ASCII garden. Plants cross-pollinate when friends
   visit with invites. Rare hybrids can be gifted.
   AR LAYER: Garden plot becomes a windowsill-scale terrarium
   on a real surface. Friends' gardens visit as spectral
   overlays on the same surface when invited. Calm, persistent.

▲ 20. LEGACY
   Async persistent · group (persistent) · social+skill · FLAGSHIP
   Long-form (months) shared fictional town. Players own
   buildings or roles; weekly council votes pick canonical
   events; retiring characters bequeath roles to newcomers.
   AR LAYER: A living scale model of the town appears on any
   flat surface. Tap a building to read its recent vignettes;
   council chamber animates during vote windows. The town's
   geometry is shared; your room is not.

▲ 29. COOPERATIVE TOWER
   Async · group (3–5) · skill+social
   Co-op puzzle vs. the system. Hidden hands, escalating
   crises, Hanabi-style comms constraints.
   AR LAYER: The crisis board sits on a table; each player sees
   their own hand in midair only in their own view. Physical
   reach-and-place gestures feel genuinely cooperative.

30. MURMUR TRAILS
    Async · 1:1 or group (2–4) · skill
    Tile-laying route/territory game. 6–24h per placement;
    games finish in 3–5 days. Great onboarding title.

▲ 35. BLOCK TOWN
   Async persistent · group (~20 per world) · skill+social
   Voxel-ish tile sandbox. Minutes-per-tick simulation. Per-plot
   permissions, rollback tools, reputation gating.
   AR LAYER: Walk through your plot at human scale or shrink it
   to tabletop for overview building. Other players visible as
   abstract silhouettes — no rooms, no real-space leakage.
   High-maintenance title; phase carefully.

▲ 37. FARM CIRCLE
   Async persistent · solo + 4–8 neighbors · skill+social
   Persistent farm on real-world calendar seasons. Neighbors
   visit, trade, leave gifts. No push notifications.
   AR LAYER: Farm occupies a designated surface in your home.
   Chores surface as small gestural tasks you can do in passing.
   Calm-tech ethos: the farm waits for you, never pings.

45. WAGER TRIVIA
    Sync · group (6–20) · skill
    Live trivia nights. Rotating categories, community-
    submitted questions with moderation. Wager rounds.
```
